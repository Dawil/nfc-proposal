Introduction

The purpose of this proposal is to explain what would be involved in using NFC chips to log attendance of BVN seminars. Topics to be covered are:

  • What we can do,
  • Who to pay,
  • What to pay,
  • Surrounding infrastructure, e.g.:
    • software involved
    • co-ordinator participation involved
  • UX,
  • and an argument for this approach.

Imagine a BVN hosted seminar on architecture styles in Sydney. There is a guest speaker giving the talk, a Futurum co-ordinator and around 15 BVN employees attending, expecting to recieve Futurum credits.

The Futurum co-ordinator needs to:

  • Identify every BVN employee attending,
  • Send every attendee an email with the relevant Epicor code and a link to a comprehension quiz,
  • Grade and log the results of every comprehension quiz attempted.

How it looks

All the Futurum participants are given a special Futurum card. All the Futurum co-ordinators are given an app on their smartphones.

When a participant arrives at a seminar they present their card to the co-ordinator who uses the app to read it. This automatically logs the attendance of the participant and also sends them a comprehension quiz to be completed for bonus points after the seminar.

Finally, both the attendance and the final quiz score for every participant is available in a human and computer readable format. This allows the co-ordinator to share the score with the participant and Futurum to log the result and award points to participants.

The experience would be similar to the video below.

How it works

There are three parts to this proposal:

  • The creation and distribution of NFC cards for each Futurum participant,
  • The creation of an Android NFC reader App for use by the Futurum co-ordinators,
  • A Futurum gmail account which will be used by the app to log attendance and quiz scores, as well as send out emails.
